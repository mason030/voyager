Voyager

A trip into unknown space

Voyager 1

Voyager 1 was the first mission to explore the outer planets, photographing Jupiter, Saturn, and their moons. Using the alignment of the planets, it was able to travel to the outer reaches of the Solar System. As it currently stands, Voyager 1 is the furthest man-made object from the Sun.

Voyager 2

Although launched sooner than Voyager 1, Voyager 2 took a longer route through the Solar System. It provided us with new imagery of Jupiter and Saturn and our first up close images of the ice giants Uranus and Neptune. All of the outer planet's primary moons were also photographed.
