Earth

September 5, 1977

Less than a month after Voyager 2 is launched, Voyager 1 is launched by nasa from the Kennedy Space Center at Merritt Island, Florida. The next day, September 6th, the first images of Earth and the Moon are taken and sent to the Jet Propulstion Laboratory (JPL) in California.

Jupiter

March 5, 1979

As Voyager 1 uses Jupiter to catapult it further out into the Solar System, it makes a number of discoveries. It discovers the importance of the Great Red Spot as a cyclonic storm on the face of Jupiter. The planet is also the first world beyond Earth known to have lightning.

Saturn

November 12, 1980

Voyager 1 encounters Saturn, discovering new moons and granting a better understanding of the planet's ring structure. It is confirmed by these newfound 'shepard' moons that they serve to keep the ring's shape. It is after its encounter with Saturn that Voyager 1 begins its path to the outer edge of the Solar System.

Rhea

August 25, 2012

Voyager 1 has crossed into interstellar space, the first man-made object to do so. This means it is no longer under the protection of the heliosphere, the zone under the Sun's magnetic field. The Voyager missions have provided decades worth of knowledge about the outer reaches of our Solar System and were a benchmark for future satellites and voyages taken up by NASA.
